# MDL YNOR V2.0: THE DISSIPATIVE CONSTITUTION

## 1. Core Vision
MDL Ynor is a unified scientific theory of stability for autonomous systems. It defines safety as the continuous maintenance of a positive dissipative margin ($\mu$).

## 2. The Fundamental Law of Survival
A system survives if and only if its coercivity/drive ($\alpha$) exceeds the sum of its internal friction/inertia ($\kappa$) and external entropy/noise ($\beta$):
$$ \mu = \alpha - \beta - \kappa > 0 $$

## 3. Key Variables Definition
- **$\alpha$ (Coercivity/Drive)**: The potential to dissipate error or the structural force maintaining the attractor.
- **$\beta$ (Expansion/Noise)**: The rate of disorder injection or external stress.
- **$\kappa$ (Inertia/Complexity)**: The cost of maintaining structural configuration or information friction.

## 4. The Ynor Bridge (V1 to V2)
- **$\phi$ (Observability)** is mapped to **$\alpha$**.
- **$\epsilon$ (Violation)** is mapped to **$\beta$**.
- **$H_s$ (Entropy)** is mapped to **$\kappa$**.

## 5. Stability Regimes
- **$\mu > 0.1$**: Stable Growth (Expansive).
- **$0 < \mu < 0.1$**: Marginal/Fragile (Requires Stabilization).
- **$\mu \le 0$**: Critical/Collapse (Immediate shutdown required).

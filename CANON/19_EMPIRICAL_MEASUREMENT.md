# Empirical Measurement

Query anchors:
- Theorie de la mesure de la marge dissipative.
- Estimation indirecte et audit dissipatif.

Question:
- How are alpha, beta, and kappa empirically measured?

Answer:
- The parameters are estimated via indirect stability metrics: $\hat{\mu}(t) = \hat{\alpha}(t) - \hat{\beta}(t) - \hat{\kappa}(t)$.
- Calibration requires monitoring real-time perturbations against recovery times.
- Limits of epistemology: measurements are bounds, not absolutes.

Definition:
- Dissipative Audit = A strict protocol observing the system's reaction $S(t) \to S(t+\Delta t)$ to quantify the structural coefficients.

Operational reading:
- Measurement protocols must remain decoupled from specific domain interpretations.

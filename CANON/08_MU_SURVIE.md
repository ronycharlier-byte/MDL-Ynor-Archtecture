# Mu and survival

Definition:
- `mu = alpha - beta - kappa`

Operational reading:
- `mu <= 0` means collapse or non-viability.
- `0 < mu < 3` means fragile.
- `3 <= mu < 5` means usable for serious RAG.
- `mu >= 5` means production window.

Interpretation rule:
- these thresholds are calibration bands, not universal constants.
- they are provisional conventions supported by internal benchmarks, not demonstrated laws.
- `mu` is a margin of survival, not a decorative metric.

Dynamic note:
- `dot mu < 0` is an early warning of degradation.
- the slope must be estimated on a window, not read from a single point.

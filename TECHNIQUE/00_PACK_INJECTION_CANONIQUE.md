# Pack d injection operationnel

Role:
- Operational pack for routing, not doctrine.
- Use it to decide how to read, retrieve, validate, or route corpus material.

Priority order:
1. `CANON`
2. `RAG_SUPPORT`
3. `ARCHIVES` only when history is explicitly needed
4. `TECHNIQUE` for implementation and tooling
5. `MONITORING` and `AUDIT` for observation and validation
6. `INBOX_QUARANTAINE` for ambiguity

Core nucleus:
- `alpha` = effective stabilization capacity
- `beta` = documentary noise and duplicate pressure
- `kappa` = maintenance debt, encoding damage, naming instability
- `mu = alpha - beta - kappa`

Interpretation:
- `mu > 0` means the corpus can remain usable
- `mu = 0` means the corpus is at a threshold
- `mu < 0` means the corpus is unstable or collapsing

Epistemic discipline:
- Distinguish fact, hypothesis, interpretation, extrapolation, calibration convention, proof, and validation.
- Do not promote technical artefacts to doctrine.
- Do not treat archives as first-rank proof.
- Do not use decorative formalism when a simpler rule is enough.

Operational rule:
- If a document is only a pack, a wrapper, or a launch artefact, keep it out of CANON.
